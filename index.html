Meet the Team

A full autonomous cycle with security and guardrails built in.

The Tech Lead

The Orchestrator

Your Tech Lead watches for new tickets on your board. When you point it at a ticket, it analyzes the requirements, sanitizes the input against prompt injection, and securely delegates the work to the Developer. It coordinates the full cycle — delegation, deployment, QA verification, and ticket closure — so you don't have to.

The Developer

The Miracle Worker

The Developer takes tickets from the Tech Lead and gets to work. Writes code, pushes to your repo, and signals when the build is ready for staging. It doesn't clone repos it shouldn't touch, doesn't deploy to production, and doesn't exceed the cost guardrails you set. When QA finds a bug, the Developer gets the encrypted fix request and tries again — up to five rounds before escalating to you.

The QA

The Gatekeeper

Once you deploy code to your staging URL, the QA agent goes to work. It hits your live endpoints, verifies responses against the acceptance criteria from the ticket, and reports a structured pass or fail with evidence. If it fails, the encrypted ticket goes back to the Developer. If it passes, the Tech Lead closes the ticket. QA never clones repos or runs servers — it only tests what's live.

Security & Guardrails

Built for Production

Every message between agents is signed with industry-standard encryption and replay protection on a durable bus. Agents fetch credentials at runtime from an encrypted secrets manager and each agent runs in its own isolated container with role-specific security policies that prevent it from reading other agents' data, deploying to production, or exceeding your cost limits. Set per-ticket, daily, and monthly model spend caps. Your code, your keys, your infrastructure — nothing leaves your server.
